Enigma hitting the headlines

Mary and Anthony Jones, founders of an art events company in the London borough of Bromley have received numerous local awards, and now they are set to receive national acclaim for their contribution to the growth, development, health and wellbeing of local communities across London. Together Mary and Anthony created an art program to give rise to health and community spirit, focusing on community building by encouraging local communities especially those who feel marginalised and isolated to, share their culture experience and worldview through art. The aim is to help individuals overcome mental health problems through the freedom of expression and self care, for local communities to engage in social activities and interaction through art. The ONS latest reports on mental health within the boroughs with which the couple's program is running has shown a huge fall in numbers; 30% of mental health patients have safely transitioned back to health under their program. 20% are back in part time work and 8% are back in full time work. Enigma are partnered with some local councils but now even major companies are now taking notice of the effect the couple's program is having on local members and are set to show their appreciation of the couple's program here's what they had to say:

Their work continues to prove essential to the community

"We absolutely love the work Enigma are doing. Mary and Anthony are real pillars of our community. When I met Mary and Anthony I was very impressed with their plans. At that time we were just recovering from Covid, lockdown restrictions and so as much as I liked their idea and plan we couldn't help or support them in anyway. Our staff had been cut, our budget had been cut, we were all overworked an under severe stress, but I was so impressed with them I was adamant we would support their cause as soon as we were able. They left my office undiscouraged and within a few months I found myself and many of my colleagues in their program. The work they have done and continue to do. continues to prove essential to the community" said Sir Robert Neil MP for Bromley and Chislehurst.

Asolutley Incredible

Sundar Pichai CEO of Google was astounded at the numbers released by the office of national statistics as it relates to the number of people that got back to work, over coming some the worst mental health conditions. He himself witnessed a large number of his own employees suffering from burn out, stress and depression, gradually all come back to work strong and healthy. he said "Working at Google can be very stressful particularly in software development and programming applications, so it is absolutely incredible what has been achieved. I will be reaching out to the founders of Enigma and seeing if we can work with them to launch their program here at Google".
