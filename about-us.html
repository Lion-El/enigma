our story

founders

We are a community support group. My partner and I formed the group on the 16th July 2023. Our inspiration to form the support group came from our grandparents. Both our grandparents live in the Caribbean. They grew up in the poorer parts together with other elders and multiple siblings. They told us many stories about what they had to endure during their childhood and most of their adult life; the trials, tribulations and trauma. Until July 2023 we hadn't seen them since we were young children, some how the stars aligned and both our grandparents came to the UK to visit our families at the same time. When we saw them we couldn't believe how happy, mentally sharp, and healthy they were. They are both in their late 80's and they looked amazing! My partner asked my Grandmother "Mama Bell what's your secret ?", she smiled and said "what secret ?", my wife replied "the secret of youth", Mama said "freedom!, no matter what is going on in life you must liberate yourself of all forms of doubt and self oppression. Just know that you are infinite, with infinite potential, so make freedom of expression you very own." My partner and I looked at each other and smiled, this was the moment the idea was planted, a few weeks later Enigma was born.

our team

dream team

Our team is amazing! We all share so much in common; a passion for community, sharing and putting others first. Ambitious, driven and empathetic. We met all our current team members at our events. Our team is so diverse they all contribute something different, which helps us to discover new and innovative ways to express yourself through art, adding real depth and richness to our program.

values

our ethos

There is so much to learn in this life and so much to be gained, from the many diverse nations, cultures, ethnic groups, their experiences and contributions. We appreciate all people no matter their background, we recognise that ultimately we are all one people with different experiences and differing ways in which we relate to the world. We believe that, in sharing our experiences through art, on the one hand enables us to feel free to face our past experiences whatever they maybe, question, analyse and communicate them in a completely abstract way, communicate our belief, faith, and traditions, our inner thoughts, all free from conflict and on the other hand gain understanding and an appreciation for how other people view and interact with the world and why. In this way we can become more connected and hopefully more tolerant, engaged and involved in each others lives.

mission

our goals

Our number one goal is to see our communities thrive and enjoy life, free from self doubt, stress and anxiety. We want to demonstrate how this can be achieved through art and the freedom of expression. We want for our communities to have open communication and be more connected, engage in social activities, sharing the richness of their cultures. We want to help those who find it mentally challenging to leave their homes, and those who struggle to socialise. We have set up multiple platforms for local community members to help with social interaction, getting back to work, over coming problems with mental health, access to therapy, access to facilities for physical exercise. Currently we have centre's set up in London across four boroughs, our aim is to increase to a level where we have a centre or a s phere of influence in every borough of London before extending nation wide.
